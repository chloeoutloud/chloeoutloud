The Internship Search That Accidentally Started This Blog

A job posting asked for a portfolio link. I didn't have one. So I made one.

Chloe Rudd·Dec 12, 2025·6 min read

Junior year internship season is its own special kind of chaos. You're simultaneously trying to keep your GPA alive, maintain some semblance of a social life, and convince strangers on the internet that you are, in fact, a competent and hireable human being. I was deep in it when I came across a job posting that changed the trajectory of my semester in a way I did not expect.

The posting was for a marketing internship — the kind that sounded genuinely interesting, not just resume-padding-interesting. At the bottom of the requirements list, almost as an afterthought, it said: "Portfolio, personal website, or blog preferred." I stared at that line for a long time. I didn't have any of those things.

"I closed the tab. Then I opened it again. Then I Googled 'how to start a blog' like someone who had absolutely no idea what they were doing — which is accurate."

The Logic That Followed

Once the idea was in my head I couldn't shake it. Why don't I have a blog? I'm studying marketing. I love writing. I have opinions constantly. That combination should have logically produced a blog years ago. The fact that it hadn't felt suddenly ridiculous.

I also thought about differentiation — something we talk about endlessly in marketing class. In a sea of junior marketing students with similar GPAs and similar club involvement, what actually sets you apart? A blog that demonstrates your voice and perspective seemed like a concrete answer.

Building a Personal Brand Before I Knew That's What I Was Doing

I didn't sit down and say "I am going to build a personal brand." That would have been too calculated and honestly too intimidating. I just started writing. I wrote about things I was thinking about, in the way I actually talk, and let it be imperfect and honest and genuinely mine.

But looking back through a marketing lens — which I can't help doing — what I was building was exactly that. A consistent voice. A defined point of view. A body of work that shows rather than just tells.

Did I Get the Internship?

I'm not going to tell you I sent the link and got the job. That's not how this story ends. But I built something I'm genuinely proud of that has made me a better writer and that I actually look forward to working on. I've included the link in every application since. The blog was the right call regardless — and that's how I know it was never really just about the internship.
