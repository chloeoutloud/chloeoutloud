Still Figuring Out Which State I Belong To

Arizona is home. Utah has become something too. I go back to Arizona for breaks and the warmth feels like a reward. I come back to Provo and the mountains remind me why I stayed. I don't think I have to choose, and writing this blog has made me realize I don't want to. I'm from Arizona and I live in Utah and both of those things are true and they're both in everything I write, whether I intend it or not.

The People Behind the Blog

My twin is my first reader. Not always by choice — sometimes I just send her a draft at 11pm and say "is this good or unhinged" and she responds honestly, which I appreciate. My friends have learned to expect occasional questions like "does this sentence sound too aggressive" at random hours. They have not complained, at least not to my face, which I choose to interpret as support.

the process

How a Post Actually Gets Made

It starts with a thought I can't stop having. I'll be in class or on a walk and something will occur to me and I'll type a note on my phone that makes zero sense to anyone but me. Then I open Google Docs — yes, I know, I wrote a whole post about this — and I just start writing. No outline, no plan. I write until it feels like something, then I go back and make it make sense. It's not a system I'd recommend, but it's mine.

Campus Life as a Junior

BYU is a strange and wonderful place. I say that with full affection. There is something about being surrounded by thousands of people who are all genuinely trying to figure out who they are and what they believe that makes for a pretty interesting atmosphere to write in. The marketing building is where I spend most of my time. The library is where I go when I need to feel productive. The Creamery is where I go when I need to feel better.

Where the Writing Actually Happens

Despite what you might picture, I do not write at a clean, aesthetic desk with soft lighting and a latte. I write at my kitchen table, usually in the two-hour window between my last class and when I have to make dinner, with whatever is left of my coffee from that morning. It's not glamorous. It's effective. The blog gets written, the coffee gets finished, and I call that a win.
