the basics

Who Is This Chick?

Hi! My name is Chloe Rudd and I am a 21 year-old BYU student. So, let's get all of the classic, generic, first-day of school questions out of the way. I am from Arizona, and have lived in Utah for the past 3 years. I'm a junior in the marketing program and I am passionate about building brands and strategy. Also, fun fact, I have an identical twin (sometimes I forget to tell people that and it takes them off guard).

I feel like it is also important to note that I am a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Days Saints. My beliefs and values will be evident in my writing, and you can expect to see an occasional post about religion. I don't love religious debate, so if you have come here to pull apart every single detail of my personal religious beliefs, it would probably be best that you leave. I'll respect your religious beliefs and point of views, so please do the same for me.

the why

Why a Blog?

The original idea actually came from a job posting. As I was applying for summer internships, one of them suggested providing a link to a blog page, portfolio, etc. At the time, I did not have a blog page, but it struck me. Why not? I enjoy writing and I have a lot of opinions. That's all you really need.

You're probably thinking to yourself, "Chloe, a blog is such an outdated form of communication, why on earth would you choose a blog to express your opinions?" To counter that I would say, "Where do you suggest I post them?" The first one that comes to mind for me is LinkedIn. So yes, I could oversaturate all of my connections with bits of writing that they genuinely do not care about, but I try to live by the golden rule. And let me tell you — if someone was flooding my LinkedIn feed with their thoughts, I am immediately removing that connection. Sorry, not sorry.

I would go on to say the same thing about my other forms of social media. If you have any other suggestions, I would love to hear them. Send them to me on LinkedIn, because I am not posting my email for the whole world — I already get enough spam as is.

The right answer

in conclusion
