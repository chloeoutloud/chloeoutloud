Moving from Arizona to Utah: A Hot Girl's Cold Reality

Three years in Provo and I still haven't fully forgiven the snow.

Chloe Rudd·Jan 10, 2026·6 min read

I grew up in Arizona, which means I grew up believing that winter was a concept that happened to other people. A season you read about in books. Something the midwest complained about. I wore flip flops in December. I thought that was normal. Then I moved to Provo, Utah at eighteen years old and the universe corrected me very swiftly and very rudely.

My first winter in Utah I genuinely did not own a coat. Not like, I owned a light jacket. I mean I did not own an actual coat. I remember texting my mom in October asking if she could mail me one and she responded "didn't I offer to get you one before you left" and I said yes but I didn't think I would actually need it. We do not speak of that first winter.

"Arizona taught me that the sun is a personality. Utah taught me that the cold is one too — and it has a lot more to say."

What I Actually Miss About Arizona

The warmth, obviously. But more specifically, the kind of warmth that feels like a hug you didn't ask for but needed anyway. Stepping outside in January back home and feeling the sun on your face like it's apologizing for nothing because it has nothing to apologize for. I miss that deeply and personally.

I miss the sunsets. Arizona sunsets are aggressive — pinks and oranges that look fake, like someone turned up the saturation on the sky. Utah has beautiful sunsets too, but they're more restrained. More tasteful. Arizona doesn't do tasteful. Arizona does dramatic and I respect that.

What Surprised Me About Loving Utah Anyway

I didn't expect to like it here. I came for school and planned to leave the second I graduated. Three years later I'm still here and if I'm being honest with myself — and this blog is kind of the whole point of being honest — I've come to love it in a way I didn't see coming.

The mountains do something to you. I don't fully understand it. I grew up surrounded by desert and cacti and I thought I was a flat-landscape person. But then I drove through Provo Canyon for the first time in fall and had some kind of spiritual experience I'm still not totally over. Utah is genuinely beautiful in a way that feels personal, like it's showing off just for you.

The Grudging Peace Treaty

I still complain about the cold. I will probably always complain about the cold. But I've stopped being surprised by it, which is progress. I own coats now — plural. I even own a real pair of snow boots, which is something I never thought I would say. Arizona will always be home. But Utah has become something too. I just wouldn't say that out loud in front of my mom.
